Effects of E-Waste on Health and the Environment

Health Effects

E-waste contains hazardous materials like lead, mercury, cadmium, and brominated flame retardants. Exposure to these substances can lead to health problems such as respiratory issues, skin diseases, and even severe conditions like cancer.

Environmental Effects

Improper disposal of e-waste can contaminate soil and water, affecting ecosystems and wildlife. Burning or landfilling e-waste releases toxic fumes and contributes to air pollution. E-waste recycling can mitigate these impacts by safely recovering valuable materials.
